Defending Public Health from D.C. to Overseas: Jane Wallis
Leighla-Marie Dantes MJFC 313 - Public Affairs Reporting 01/23/2026

Jane Wallis is the Executive Director of Grassroots Health, Community Co-Chair of the D.C. Regional Planning Commission on Health and HIV (COHAH), and a prominent public health advocate in Washington D.C., who has devoted over a decade of time, resources and effort to protecting the District's health and safety. From overseas in Zambia to locally in the United States, Wallis has displayed a commitment to being an educator and changemaker in the world of public health. D.C. is facing ongoing public health challenges related to youth mental health, sexual health education gaps, and health inequities, particularly in underserved wards. As the D.C. Council’s Committee on Health continues to examine public health funding, youth wellness initiatives, and HIV prevention strategies, Wallis’s work offers a real-time example of how community-based organizations influence outcomes and policy beyond government agencies. As executive legislation regarding reproductive rights and freedoms changes, it is important that figures such as Wallis are active and committed to serving a community that can fall through the cracks. There has also been a noticeable scrutiny of youth mental health resources post-pandemic.
